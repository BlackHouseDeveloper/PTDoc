Pelvic Floor limitations/objectives/Goals

#### **Mobility & Transfers**

* Difficulty standing or walking for prolonged periods due to pelvic discomfort

* Limited ability to climb stairs or ramps because of pelvic pain or instability

* Difficulty transitioning from sitting to standing or vice versa without pain

* Trouble getting in and out of bed or car due to pelvic pain or tightness

* Difficulty squatting, kneeling, or bending due to pelvic floor muscle tension or pain

#### **Self-Care / Activities of Daily Living (ADLs)**

* Difficulty with toileting, including sitting on or rising from the toilet

* Pain or discomfort during wiping or hygiene tasks post-toileting

* Challenges with sexual activity, including pain during intercourse or inability to engage comfortably

* Difficulty with bathing or showering due to pain when positioning or moving

* Difficulty dressing lower body due to pelvic or hip discomfort

#### **Bowel and Bladder Function**

* Urinary urgency, frequency, or incontinence impacting ability to perform tasks uninterrupted

* Constipation or straining leading to pain and limited bowel movements

* Difficulty controlling or delaying bowel movements (fecal urgency or incontinence)

* Pain during urination or bowel movements causing avoidance behaviors

* Inability to complete toileting tasks independently due to pain or weakness

#### **Work-Related Functional Limitations**

* Difficulty standing or sitting for prolonged periods at workstation due to pelvic discomfort

* Reduced tolerance for lifting, bending, or carrying heavy objects because of pelvic pain or instability

* Limited ability to perform repetitive tasks involving trunk or core muscles

* Challenges operating machinery or driving due to pelvic or lower abdominal pain

#### **Household and Leisure Activities**

* Difficulty performing household chores requiring bending, lifting, or prolonged standing

* Avoidance or inability to participate in recreational activities such as exercise, sports, or dancing due to pelvic pain

* Difficulty with gardening, yard work, or other activities requiring squatting or kneeling

* Reduced ability to travel or attend social events due to pain flare-ups or urgency

#### **Psychosocial and Emotional Impact**

* Increased anxiety or fear related to pain flare-ups or functional limitations

* Avoidance of intimate or social interactions due to discomfort or embarrassment

* Sleep disturbances due to pain or urgency impacting overall functioning

Goals

**Short-Term Goals (2–4 weeks):**

* Patient will stand for 10 minutes without pelvic pain increase during physical therapy session by week 3\.

* Patient will transition from sitting to standing independently with minimal pelvic discomfort in 2 weeks.

* Patient will climb 3 stairs using handrail with reduced pelvic pain within 4 weeks.

**Long-Term Goals (6–8 weeks):**

* Patient will stand and walk for 30 minutes without pelvic pain flare-up in community setting within 8 weeks.

* Patient will transfer in and out of bed or car independently and without pelvic pain in 6 weeks.

* Patient will climb a flight of stairs reciprocally without pelvic pain or need for support by 8 weeks.

---

### **Self-Care / Activities of Daily Living (ADLs)**

**Short-Term Goals (2–4 weeks):**

* Patient will perform sit-to-stand from toilet with minimal pelvic discomfort and without external support in 3 weeks.

* Patient will safely complete hygiene tasks post-toileting without pain flare-up in 2 weeks.

* Patient will dress lower body while seated, reducing pain episodes by 50% within 4 weeks.

**Long-Term Goals (6–8 weeks):**

* Patient will complete full toileting transfer and hygiene independently and without pelvic pain in 6 weeks.

* Patient will bathe/shower safely and independently standing for 10 minutes without pelvic discomfort by 8 weeks.

* Patient will dress lower body standing without pain or need for frequent rest in 7 weeks.

---

### **Bowel and Bladder Function**

**Short-Term Goals (2–4 weeks):**

* Patient will report decreased urinary urgency episodes by 30% within 3 weeks.

* Patient will perform pelvic floor muscle relaxation techniques to reduce pain during urination or bowel movements by 4 weeks.

* Patient will achieve successful timed toileting schedule 80% of the time in 2 weeks to reduce urgency episodes.

**Long-Term Goals (6–8 weeks):**

* Patient will maintain continence with no leakage episodes during daily activities in 8 weeks.

* Patient will report pain-free urination and bowel movements consistently by 7 weeks.

* Patient will perform pelvic floor muscle strengthening and relaxation exercises independently at home with 90% adherence in 8 weeks.

---

### **Work-Related Functional Limitations**

**Short-Term Goals (2–4 weeks):**

* Patient will stand at workstation for 15 minutes without pelvic pain or need to shift weight in 3 weeks.

* Patient will perform light lifting tasks (\<10 lbs) with proper pelvic mechanics and minimal pain within 4 weeks.

* Patient will tolerate sitting for 30 minutes at work with pelvic pain managed to \<3/10 in 2 weeks.

**Long-Term Goals (6–8 weeks):**

* Patient will stand or sit at workstation for 60 minutes without pelvic pain flare-up or fatigue in 8 weeks.

* Patient will lift and carry objects up to 25 lbs with proper pelvic stability and no pain in 7 weeks.

* Patient will perform work duties including bending and reaching without pelvic pain or limitation in 8 weeks.

---

### **Household and Leisure Activities**

**Short-Term Goals (2–4 weeks):**

* Patient will stand and complete cooking tasks for 10 minutes without pelvic pain flare-up in 3 weeks.

* Patient will perform light household chores (e.g., dusting) for 10 minutes without pelvic discomfort in 4 weeks.

* Patient will tolerate sitting during leisure activities without pelvic pain in 2 weeks.

**Long-Term Goals (6–8 weeks):**

* Patient will perform full meal preparation standing for 20 minutes without pelvic pain in 6 weeks.

* Patient will participate in leisure activities such as walking or gardening for 30 minutes without pelvic pain or fatigue in 8 weeks.

* Patient will engage in social outings or events with standing and walking for 30 minutes without pelvic pain or urgency in 7 weeks.

---

### **Psychosocial and Emotional Impact**

**Short-Term Goals (2–4 weeks):**

* Patient will verbalize decreased anxiety related to pelvic pain by 30% using a pain/anxiety scale within 4 weeks.

* Patient will identify and use 2 coping strategies for pain flare-ups during therapy sessions within 3 weeks.

**Long-Term Goals (6–8 weeks):**

* Patient will report improved quality of life related to pelvic pain management on validated questionnaire by 7 weeks.

* Patient will demonstrate regular use of coping and relaxation techniques independently to manage pain and anxiety in 8 weeks.
