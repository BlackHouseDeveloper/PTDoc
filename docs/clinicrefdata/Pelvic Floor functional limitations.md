**Pelvic Floor functional limitations** 

**Urinary incontinence** — inability to control urine, leading to leakage during activities like coughing, sneezing, or exercise

**Urinary urgency and frequency** — frequent need to urinate or sudden, intense urges that are hard to control

**Incomplete bladder emptying** — feeling of residual urine after voiding, leading to repeated trips to the bathroom

**Fecal incontinence** — involuntary loss of stool or gas

**Constipation or difficulty with bowel movements** — straining, incomplete evacuation, or painful defecation

**Pelvic organ prolapse** — sensation of bulging or pressure in the vaginal, rectal, or pelvic area that interferes with daily activities

**Pain with intercourse (dyspareunia)** — discomfort or pain during or after sexual activity

**Pelvic or lower abdominal pain** — chronic or intermittent discomfort affecting mobility or posture

**Difficulty with sitting or prolonged standing** — pain or discomfort limiting ability to remain in one position

**Reduced core stability** — impaired support for trunk and posture, affecting balance and movement

**Difficulty performing heavy lifting or strenuous activities** — fear of leakage or prolapse worsening

**Impaired ability to perform exercises or physical activities** — due to pain, weakness, or fear of symptoms

**Difficulty with transfers and mobility** — pain or weakness limiting getting in/out of bed, chairs, or cars

**Sleep disruption** — frequent nighttime urination or pain interfering with restful sleep

**Difficulty with hygiene or self-care activities** — due to incontinence or pain

**Emotional distress and social withdrawal** — avoiding social situations or exercise due to embarrassment or fear of symptoms

**Reduced work productivity or absenteeism** — symptoms impacting concentration or attendance

**Difficulty with balance or gait changes** — secondary to pelvic instability or pain

**Inability to tolerate tight clothing or certain postures** — due to pain or pressure

**Impaired respiratory function** — due to altered breathing patterns secondary to pelvic floor dysfunction
