## Trigger Point Dry Needling Treatment Consent Form

**I, January Austin** do hereby, ☑ **give my voluntary consent** ☐ **do
not consent** **for the administration of trigger point dry needling.**

Trigger point dry needling technique is a treatment used for the purpose
of alleviating pain and improving posture and movement. It uses fine,
solid filament needles with or without the application of electrical
stimulation. This clinic uses sterile, single use, disposable needles
and maintains a clean and safe environment. The needles are inserted
through the skin into the underlying tissues and muscles at specific
points known as myofascial trigger points. When a twitch response is
obtained, the trigger point is released. Manual therapy techniques are
often incorporated into treatment. I understand that trigger point dry
needling is not a form of acupuncture.

I have been informed that trigger point dry needling is generally a safe
method of treatment, but that it may have side effects, including
bruising, post treatment soreness and discomfort, and in rare cases,
dizziness or fainting. While the risk of trigger point dry needling is
small, there have been very rare instances reported of pneumo-thorax or
a collapsed lung. I understand that while this document describes the
major risks of treatment, other side effects may occur. Alternative
methods of treatment and their benefits and risks have been explained to
me.

I will notify Physically Fit Physical Therapy, Inc and the treating
therapist(s) should I have a bleeding disorder, take anti-coagulants
(blood thinners), have a pacemaker or defibrillator, have any implants
(medical or cosmetic) or am pregnant. If any of these conditions arise
during the course of my treatment, I will notify Physically Fit Physical
Therapy, Inc and the treating therapist(s) immediately of the change in
my status.

By signing below, I give my consent to evaluation and treatment. I
understand that I can refuse treatment at any time. I have been told
about the risks and benefits of trigger point dry needling and have had

## **Purpose of Pelvic Health Physical Therapy**

Pelvic health physical therapy focuses on the evaluation and treatment
of conditions related to the pelvic region, including pelvic pain,
incontinence, prolapse, sexual dysfunction, and other musculoskeletal
and neuromuscular issues affecting the pelvic floor, hips, and lower
abdomen.

## **Description of Pelvic Examinations**

As part of your evaluation and treatment, the physical therapist may
perform both **external** and **internal pelvic examinations** to assess
muscles, connective tissues, nerve function, and overall pelvic health.
These exams help to identify areas of dysfunction and guide effective
treatment planning.

-   **External exam:** Visual and manual assessment of the pelvic,
    abdominal, and hip regions, including palpation of muscles and
    connective tissue.

-   **Internal exam:** A manual examination performed internally via the
    vagina or rectum (as appropriate), to assess pelvic floor muscle
    function, tone, strength, and tenderness.

## **Benefits**

-   Accurate diagnosis of pelvic floor dysfunction

-   Improved individualized treatment planning

-   Potential for symptom relief and functional improvement

## **Risks and Discomforts**

-   Some patients may experience mild discomfort, pressure, or temporary
    soreness during or after the exam.

-   Rarely, emotional discomfort or anxiety may occur due to the
    intimate nature of the exam.

-   Please inform your therapist immediately if you experience
    significant pain or distress.

## **Your Rights**

-   You have the right to **refuse or withdraw consent** for any part of
    the examination or treatment at any time without affecting your
    future care.

-   You have the right to request a **chaperone** or a same-gender
    therapist if preferred.

-   You may ask questions about the procedure or request further
    information at any time.

## **Confidentiality**

All information obtained during the examination and treatment is
confidential and will be protected according to HIPAA guidelines and
professional ethical standards.
