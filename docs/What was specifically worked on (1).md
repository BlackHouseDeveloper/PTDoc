Things to address by joint

## **Foot & Ankle**

* Ankle dorsiflexion/plantarflexion mobility

* Subtalar joint inversion/eversion control

* Midfoot mobility/stiffness (e.g., cuboid/navicular glide)

* Toe flexor/extensor strength

* Ankle proprioception & balance

* Talocrural joint arthrokinematics (e.g., posterior glide of talus)

* Arch support & dynamic foot posture

* Gait pattern / push-off mechanics

* Achilles tendon loading

---

## **Knee**

* Patellar tracking & alignment

* Tibiofemoral joint arthrokinematics (e.g., screw-home mechanism)

* Quad control & activation (VMO targeting, etc.)

* Hamstring-quadriceps strength ratio

* Joint mobility (flexion/extension ROM)

* Knee valgus/varus dynamic control

* Meniscal loading tolerance

* Terminal knee extension

* Proximal control influence (hip to knee)

---

## **Hip**

* Hip arthrokinematics (e.g., posterior glide for flexion)

* Lateral pelvic control (Trendelenburg)

* Glute med/max activation & recruitment

* Hip flexor length & control (iliopsoas, rectus femoris)

* Hip rotation control (IR/ER)

* Femoral head positioning (centering)

* SI joint mobility/stability

* Lumbopelvic rhythm coordination

* Piriformis syndrome management

---

## **Pelvis / SI Joint**

* SI joint mobility/stability

* Sacral nutation/counternutation control

* Lumbopelvic rhythm during movement

* Core-lumbopelvic coordination

* Pubic symphysis stability

* Pelvic floor activation/dysfunction

---

## **Lumbar Spine**

* Lumbar segmental mobility

* Core activation & bracing (transversus abdominis, multifidus)

* Flexion vs extension bias assessment

* Lumbopelvic rhythm (esp. with hip hinge or sit-to-stand)

* Facet joint mobility

* Traction response (manual or mechanical)

* Neural mobility (sciatic, femoral nerve tension testing)

* Directional preference (e.g., McKenzie)

* Pain centralization strategies

---

## **Thoracic Spine**

* Thoracic extension & rotation mobility

* Thoracic spine & rib cage expansion (breathing mechanics)

* Scapulothoracic rhythm

* Postural control (kyphosis correction, etc.)

* Segmental mobility & manipulation/mobilization

* Regional interdependence with shoulder/cervical/lumbar

---

## **Cervical Spine**

* Cervical joint mobility (flexion/extension/rotation/side-bend)

* Cervical arthrokinematics (e.g., facet glide, OA joint)

* Cervical traction (manual/mechanical)

* Deep neck flexor activation

* Cervical proprioception (joint position error testing)

* Cervicogenic headache treatment

* Thoracic mobility influence on cervical loading

---

## **Shoulder**

* Glenohumeral arthrokinematics (anterior/posterior/inferior glide)

* Scapulohumeral rhythm

* Rotator cuff strength & neuromuscular control

* Scapular stabilization (serratus anterior, lower trap)

* Shoulder elevation pattern (impingement vs dyskinesis)

* Shoulder IR/ER strength ratio

* Posterior capsule mobility

* Joint mobilization (Grades I–IV)

* Thoracic spine influence

---

## **Elbow**

* Elbow flexion/extension mobility

* Valgus stress control (throwing mechanics, UCL issues)

* Joint arthrokinematics (radiohumeral, ulnohumeral)

* Grip strength & forearm muscle endurance

* Neural mobility (radial, ulnar, median)

* Epicondylitis protocols (eccentrics, bracing)

---

## **Wrist & Hand**

* Wrist joint mobility (flexion, extension, radial/ulnar deviation)

* Carpal glide techniques (e.g., lunate, scaphoid)

* Median/ulnar nerve gliding

* Thumb opposition & stability (CMC joint)

* Grip strength & pinch strength

* Fine motor control & dexterity

* Tendon gliding exercises (FDS/FDP, EPL)

---

## **Neuromuscular & Regional Concepts**

* Regional interdependence (e.g., hip → knee, thoracic → shoulder)

* Proprioception & balance strategies (e.g., Y-Balance Test)

* Motor control retraining (e.g., lumbo-pelvic control)

* Movement pattern analysis (SFMA/FMS)

* Core-to-extremity sequencing

* Breathing mechanics integration (diaphragm vs accessory muscles)
